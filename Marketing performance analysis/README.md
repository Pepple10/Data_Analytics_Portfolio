**Marketing Campaign Performance Analysis**

**Overview**



This project analyzes customer purchase behavior and marketing campaign performance using a structured marketing dataset. The objective is to evaluate how different marketing channels, customer segments, and product categories contribute to revenue and conversion outcomes.



**Objectives**

* Assess revenue performance across marketing channels
* Evaluate campaign effectiveness using conversion rates
* Identify high-performing product categories
* Understand customer distribution by age group and location
* Track revenue trends over time



**Data Cleaning \& Preparation**



The dataset was cleaned and transformed using Excel and Power BI:



* Converted text-based dates into proper date format
* Handled missing values in Age and City fields
* Standardized text fields (names, categories, channels)
* Removed duplicate customer entries
* Created derived fields:
* Age Group
* Month
* High Value Customer flag



Identified and handled invalid email formats



**Key Metrics**



The analysis focused on the following measures:



* Total Revenue
* Total Customers
* Conversion Rate
* Average Purchase Value



**Dashboard Overview**



The Power BI dashboard includes:



* KPI cards for high-level performance tracking
* Revenue breakdown by marketing channel
* Conversion rate comparison across channels
* Product category revenue distribution
* Customer segmentation by age group
* Geographic distribution of customers
* Monthly revenue trend analysis



Interactive slicers were added for:



* Marketing Channel
* Product Category
* City



**Key Insights**



* Certain marketing channels generate significantly higher revenue but may not have the highest conversion rates
* A small segment of high-value customers contributes disproportionately to total revenue
* Specific product categories consistently outperform others in revenue generation
* Younger and middle-aged customer groups show stronger engagement and purchasing activity
* Revenue trends reveal periods of growth and potential seasonal patterns
* Customer concentration is higher in specific cities, indicating key target markets



**Recommendations**

* Allocate more budget to high-performing marketing channels with strong revenue output
* Optimize underperforming channels by improving targeting or messaging
* Focus marketing efforts on high-value customer segments to maximize returns
* Promote top-performing product categories more aggressively
* Leverage geographic insights to target high-density cities with localized campaigns
* Use time-based trends to plan campaigns around peak performance periods



Tools Used

* Excel (Data Cleaning)
* Power BI (Data Modeling \& Visualization)



**Conclusion**



This analysis provides a structured view of marketing performance and customer behavior, enabling data-driven decision-making to improve campaign effectiveness and revenue generation.
